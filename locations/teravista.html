Teravista | Round Rock, Williamson County TX

Leak Detection & Repair in Teravista, TX

Teravista is one of Round Rock's larger master-planned communities, built from the early 2000s through the 2010s along the Lake Creek Greenbelt. It mixes single-family homes, townhomes, and resort-style amenities, and its newer construction carries the pipe materials typical of that era: PVC, PEX, and late-era copper.

Newer construction, first slab failures

Teravista homes built between 2002 and 2012 are now in their teens to early twenties, the age window when the first under-slab supply failures begin to appear. PVC glued joints in the slab that were set under construction pressure show slow weeps at the couplings. Copper supply in homes from that era has accumulated nearly two decades of hard-water pitting at the elbows. The symptom is usually the meter: it turns slowly with everything off, or the bill climbs while water use did not change.

Slab leaks in Teravista are found non-invasively. We use acoustic correlation and thermal imaging to locate the break before a core goes into the floor. Call (512) 737-6168 if the meter is moving when nothing should be on.

Pool and spa leaks in Teravista

Teravista's amenity-rich lots mean a higher-than-average share of homes with inground pools and attached spas. Pool plumbing from the early 2000s build-out is now old enough for the buried return and suction lines to develop failures at the joints. The pool shell penetrations at the skimmer and lights are another source of slow loss. A pool that drops faster than evaporation explains, or a spa that empties while the pool holds steady, points at the plumbing or the shell.

We pressure-test buried pool lines and use dye testing on the shell to isolate exactly which component is leaking before any deck is opened. Call (512) 737-6168 if a pool or spa is losing water.

Irrigation in a large master-planned footprint

Teravista's larger lot sizes and the greenbelt-adjacent properties come with full irrigation systems that cover significant ground. Those systems now carry early-2000s zone valves and manifold connections that weep at the solenoids and fittings. A full irrigation pressure test per zone isolates which lateral is leaking underground, and EM pipe tracing maps the run before any digging. We locate the break precisely and repair one section rather than tearing up a landscape to search.

Getting a Teravista leak handled

Whether it is a slab leak under a newer tile floor, a pool that will not hold its level, or an irrigation zone that climbs the bill every summer, the approach is the same. Confirm the source non-invasively, locate it precisely, and make one targeted repair at the confirmed spot. Teravista's newer construction means the pipe around any given failure is usually still sound, so a repair holds. For any leak in Teravista, call (512) 737-6168 and we can get there today.
