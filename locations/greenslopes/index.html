Leak Detection & Repair in Greenslopes, TX

Greenslopes is an established Round Rock neighborhood on the eastern side of the city, where the Blackland Prairie clay soil dominates. Homes here sit on expansive clay that swells with moisture and shrinks in drought. That movement affects the plumbing under and around the foundation in ways that differ from the more stable limestone west side.

What expansive clay does to plumbing

Blackland Prairie clay moves with moisture content. During a wet spring it swells; during a summer drought it pulls back. That cycle exerts lateral force on a slab and on the pipes embedded in it, working the copper at the bends and transitions over years of movement. A slab leak in a Greenslopes home is often not a pipe failure in isolation but the result of cumulative stress at a point where the clay movement concentrated. Stage 2 drought conditions in 2026 have accelerated the shrink cycle for many lots in this area.

If your floor has a warm spot or the meter is turning slowly overnight, call (512) 737-6168 and we can evaluate whether the clay is the factor.

Foundation and below-grade drain leaks

The expansive clay that stresses a slab can also shift a sewer lateral or a below-grade drain enough to separate a joint or crack the pipe at a low point. A cracked lateral in a clay-soil yard loses water into the ground without surfacing, quietly feeding the clay further and potentially accelerating the next cycle of movement. The camera also shows any belly or displaced joint before it becomes a blockage. We camera the lateral to see joint condition and slope, and we check the cleanout for any standing water that signals a belly or a blockage at a shifted section.

Kitchen and bath drain leaks in Greenslopes homes

The kitchen and bathroom drain connections in 2000s Greenslopes construction are at the age where the PVC slip-joint connections and the waste arm fittings show their first slow weeps. A cabinet under the kitchen sink that smells musty or a floor near the bathroom that feels soft are the surface signs. We check the trap, the cleanout connections, and the waste arm before opening anything, because the drain side leaks only when water runs and the source is rarely at the most obvious spot.

Greenslopes leak service

Greenslopes leaks often have a soil-movement component that makes the diagnostic phase more involved than in geologically stable neighborhoods. We account for the clay behavior when interpreting where the meter loss is going and where the pipe is most stressed. The repair targets the confirmed failure and we note any structural context, such as the direction of soil movement or the slope of the drain run, that might affect where the next failure appears.
