North Austin and its pipe ages

The north Austin neighborhoods nearest to Round Rock carry a mix of pipe ages that closely mirrors what we see across the Round Rock area, which means the same detection methods and repair approaches apply. The older 1970s and 1980s ranch-style homes in north Austin run copper supply that is now 40 to 50 years old, approaching the end of its reliable service range under hard water. Those homes see the highest rate of slab leaks and pinhole failures. The 1990s and 2000s builds in the northern growth corridors carry copper that is 20 to 30 years old and entering the first-failure window. Call (512) 737-6168 if the meter is moving or the ceiling is staining.

Slab leaks in Austin homes

Austin is predominantly slab-on-grade construction, and slab leaks are one of the most common plumbing failures we see from Austin customers. The hard water from the Colorado River system and the age of the copper in older north Austin homes accelerate pitting at the bends and couplings under the slab. Central Texas thermal cycling does the rest. We locate Austin slab leaks with acoustic correlation and thermal imaging, the same non-invasive approach we use across Williamson County. The concrete is not opened until we know exactly where the core needs to go.
