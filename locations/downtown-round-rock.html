Leak Detection & Repair in Downtown Round Rock, TX

Downtown Round Rock blends historic storefronts with newer mixed-use buildings and the commercial corridor along Mays Street. Plumbing here ranges from aging systems in century-old blocks to modern multi-tenant builds, and a leak in either costs business hours.

Commercial and mixed-use plumbing downtown

The buildings along Round Rock Avenue and the side streets off the square carry decades of plumbing renovations layered on top of each other. An original copper supply from a 1970s remodel feeds a fixture added in the 1990s, with a PVC repair spliced in last decade. Those layered systems develop leaks at transition joints and at the oldest sections of pipe, and tracking one down requires sorting out which generation of plumbing is at fault.

We work in occupied commercial spaces and schedule detection visits before or after hours where a water shutoff during business hours is not an option. Call (512) 737-6168 and we can talk through the timing.

The Dell Diamond and Round Rock Express neighborhood

The area surrounding Dell Diamond, which opened in 2000 and anchors the north side of downtown, brought a wave of commercial development that now carries supply and sewer lines in their mid-twenties. That age puts them at the beginning of the period when irrigation connections and under-slab supply begins to show its first failures. A slow rise in the water bill, a soft patch in a parking lot, or a wall in a nearby office space that stays damp are the usual early signs.

Slab leaks under commercial flooring are a business interruption, not just a repair. We locate non-invasively first so the core goes in exactly once. Call (512) 737-6168 for a commercial leak assessment.

Water hardness and downtown plumbing

Downtown Round Rock draws from the same Lake Travis supply via BCRUA that serves the rest of the city, with water running near 15.2 grains per gallon. That hardness is hard on the cartridges in commercial faucets, on the spray arms in restaurant dishwashers, and on any copper supply line that has been carrying it for decades. Scale builds inside the pipe bore and at every fitting, and eventually the scale is what holds a failing joint together until it lets go.

We clear scale-related leaks at the source and check the pressure regulator valve, since high street pressure downtown accelerates every failure downstream.

What to expect when we arrive

For a commercial property we start with the meter and establish whether the loss is pressurized or drain-side. Then we work non-invasively so your floor and walls stay intact until we are certain where the core needs to go. We document what failed and what was repaired, which property managers and building owners need for their records. Repairs happen around your hours where possible.
