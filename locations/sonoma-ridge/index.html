Hard water on the limestone side of Round Rock

Round Rock's water runs near 15.2 grains per gallon citywide, but homes on the limestone west side experience the effects more directly in their outdoor plumbing and slab. The rocky subgrade means water from a buried leak drains fast rather than surfacing, so the only sign is the meter. An underground supply leak at 80 psi can lose hundreds of gallons a week into the limestone without ever making the yard wet. The meter test is how we confirm it before any digging starts.

If your meter is running with nothing on inside the house, call (512) 737-6168 and we can confirm and locate the loss.

Slab and supply failures in 2000s homes

Sonoma Ridge homes built in the mid-2000s are now in their high-stress decade for under-slab copper. The combination of thermal cycling, hard water pitting from the inside, and occasional drought-related soil movement stresses the copper at the bends and couplings in these limestone-zone homes. A warm spot on a tile floor, a meter that turns with everything off, or a water bill that climbs without explanation are all worth investigating.

We use acoustic correlation and thermal imaging to locate slab leaks in Sonoma Ridge before any concrete is opened. Call (512) 737-6168 for a non-invasive assessment.

Pool and outdoor plumbing in Sonoma Ridge

Several Sonoma Ridge properties include inground pools with buried return and suction lines running through the rocky subgrade. Those lines fail at the fittings and at shell penetrations as the construction ages. A pool that drops faster than evaporation explains, or a skimmer that never quite reaches full suction pressure, points at the buried plumbing. Both symptoms show up in pools from the early 2000s build-out across Sonoma Ridge. We pressure-test each buried pool line separately and locate the break with acoustic equipment before opening any section of deck.
