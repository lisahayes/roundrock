Pool Liner Leak Detection and Repair in Round Rock, TX

A vinyl liner is the waterproof skin of the pool, and it leaks where that skin is broken: a puncture, a split seam, or the cut edges where fittings pass through it. Finding the exact break is what makes the patch hold.

How a liner leaks

A vinyl liner fails in a few familiar ways. A puncture or tear from a dropped object or a sharp root lets water straight through. A factory or aged seam can split along its line. And every fitting cut into the liner, the skimmer faceplate, the returns, the steps and the main drain, relies on a gasket and clamped vinyl that can weep as the material ages and shrinks. Sun and years stiffen the vinyl until small movements open a gap.

Liners in Behrens Ranch and other established neighborhoods are now old enough that the fittings and seams are the parts giving way.

Pinpointing the break

A liner leak is found in the water, not from the deck. With the pool still, we release a thread of dye near each suspected spot and watch where the water draws it in, since a real tear pulls the dye through. We work the fittings, the seams, the steps, and any visible crease methodically, marking each confirmed point. A liner can have more than one break, so we check the whole skin rather than stopping at the first.

If your liner is losing water and you cannot see why, call (512) 737-6168 and we can dye-test it properly.

Patch or reline

A clean tear or a leaking seam in a sound liner takes an underwater vinyl patch, cut and bonded so it stays down for the long run rather than lifting at the edges. A weeping fitting gets a fresh gasket and a correctly clamped faceplate. The honest line is age: when the vinyl has gone brittle and is splitting in several places, patches become a losing game, and a new liner is the repair that actually lasts. We tell you which side of that line your liner is on.

When a reline is the answer, the base under the new liner matters as much as the vinyl. We check the floor and walls behind the old liner for washouts and soft spots, since a fresh liner laid over a hollow base will only fail again at the same place. A sound base and a properly fitted liner are what turn a reline into a long-term fix.

Why liner leaks spread when ignored

A small liner leak rarely stays small. Water passing through a tear washes out the sand or vermiculite base behind the liner, leaving a void that lets the tear stretch and the liner sag. Once the base is undermined, even a new patch has nothing solid to seal against. The escaping water also keeps the ground around the pool soft. Catching a liner leak while it is one clean tear keeps the fix to a patch, not a reline plus a base repair.

A note on hard water and vinyl

Round Rock's hard water leaves its mark on a liner too. Minerals near 15.2 grains per gallon deposit a scale line at the water level and stiffen the vinyl over the years, and stiffer vinyl tears more easily at a fitting or a crease. Balanced water and steady levels help a liner last, while a pool that is constantly refilled from a leak ages its own liner faster. We point that out so a repair is not undone by the same cycle that caused it. Call (512) 737-6168 to get it looked at.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you find a hole in a pool liner?

With the water still, we release dye near suspected spots and watch where it gets pulled through. A real tear draws the dye in. We check fittings, seams, and steps across the whole liner.

Can a vinyl liner be patched underwater?

Yes. A clean tear or seam in an otherwise sound liner takes an underwater vinyl patch bonded to stay down. A brittle liner splitting in several places is better replaced.

Why does my liner keep leaking after a patch?

Often the base behind the liner has washed out, so the patch has nothing solid behind it, or there is a second tear. We check the whole liner and the base, not just the first spot.
