Frequently Asked Questions

What are the signs of a slab leak in Round Rock TX?

The most reliable signs are a water meter that keeps moving with all fixtures off, warm or hot spots on the floor, the sound of running water inside the slab, and a water bill that spiked without explanation. Damp or buckled flooring and musty smells at the baseboard are later-stage signs that usually mean the leak has been running for some time.

Can clay soil cause slab leaks in Round Rock?

Clay soil is a contributing factor, not a direct cause. Many Round Rock subdivisions sit on expansive Blackland Prairie clay that swells when wet and shrinks during drought. That seasonal movement stresses the copper supply lines and drain connections embedded in the slab over time, making leaks more likely as the pipes age.

How do plumbers find a slab leak without breaking concrete?

Acoustic sensors listen for the frequency of pressurized water escaping through a pipe. Electronic line tracing maps the exact path of the pipe. Thermal cameras detect the temperature difference a hot-water leak creates on the floor surface. These methods pinpoint the leak so that concrete is only opened at the confirmed spot.

Does a slab leak raise the water bill?

Yes. A slab leak runs continuously under the foundation and can add 8 to 40 gallons per hour to your consumption. At that rate, monthly usage rises into Tier 2 or higher on the Round Rock schedule, and the winter sewer impact adds a second penalty if the leak runs between November and February.
