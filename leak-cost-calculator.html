Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a dripping faucet cost per year in Round Rock TX?

A faucet dripping at 60 drips per minute wastes roughly 5,400 gallons per year. At Round Rock Tier 1 rates that adds about $14 per year. If your household already uses over 10,000 gallons per month and the drip pushes you into Tier 2 at $3.63 per 1,000 gallons, the cost is higher. Use the calculator above to see your exact tier.

Does Round Rock have tiered water pricing?

Yes. The City of Round Rock uses a five-tier volumetric schedule for the 5/8-inch residential meter. Tier 1 covers 0 to 10,000 gallons at $2.57 per 1,000, then the rate steps up through Tier 5 at $7.96 per 1,000 gallons for usage over 34,000 gallons per month.

Does a winter leak raise my Round Rock sewer bill?

Yes. Round Rock bills residential sewer using a winter average of your water consumption in November, December, January, and February. A leak during those months raises that average and increases your sewer charge at $3.21 per 1,000 gallons for the following 12 months.

Can I get a leak adjustment on my Round Rock utility bill?

The City of Round Rock offers a Leak Adjustment program for customers who have fixed a confirmed leak. You can submit a request at roundrocktexas.gov. An invoice or plumber documentation showing the repair is typically required.
